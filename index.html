Rubin ToO 2024: Envisioning the Vera C. Rubin Observatory LSST Target of Opportunity program

Berkeley, March 18-20, 2024

Rationale

The Legacy Survey of Space and Time (LSST) at Vera C. Rubin Observatory is set to start in the next couple of years. The LSST survey cadence is designed in an interative community driven process (Bianco+2021). The implementation of a Target of Opportunity (ToO) program using up to 3% of the LSST sky time has been recommanded by the Survey Cadence Optimization Committee (SCOC; see PSTN-055). This program is recommanded to initially respond to Gravitationl Wave triggers, but with the potential to expand to other phenomena of interest. This workshop will bring together experts from the scientific community, Rubin Observatory personnel, and member of the Rubin SCOC to deliver a recommendation for the implementation of the ToO program. These include: how to select and prioritize triggers, automate response to triggers, and what exposures, filters, and cadence are required.

The workshop is designed with plenary discussion to explain the boundary conditions for ToOs set by the Observatory, and collaborative breakouts to define the neads of identified scientific cases, and develop trigger and prioritization schemes.

Goal

The Rubin ToO 2024 workshop will bring together members of the community with scientific interest and knowledge in ToO observations to make recommendations to the SCOC of LSST. The product of this workshop will be a document delivered to the SCOC at the end of the workshop.
